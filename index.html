About Me

I am a climate resilience specialist with local government experience and expertise in municipal sustainability projects, stakeholder engagement, and climate action. My work spans from urban climate initiatives to sustainable agriculture systems.

I am particularly interested in equitable approaches to climate resilience in coastal and agricultural communities. I believe that relationship building and co-learning are essential for supporting communities as they implement sustainable practices and adapt to climate change impacts.

Through collaborative partnerships and community-centered approaches, I work to develop resilience strategies that are both scientifically grounded and locally responsive, ensuring that climate solutions serve the diverse needs of the communities most affected by environmental change.

Research Areas

🌍 Environmental Justice

My journey into environmental justice began with undergraduate studies in biological conservation and agroecology. Through NSF-funded research on climate adaptation in Ugandan bean farms and work testing water quality for agricultural nutrients, I became increasingly interested in the impacts of industrial agriculture on surrounding communities and the trade-offs farmers face. This led me to the University of Michigan's School for Environment and Sustainability, where I focused on water governance, equitable climate adaptation, energy justice, and Indigenous rights.

🌊 Climate Resilience

Through my graduate research and positions with the Cooperative Institute for Great Lakes Research (CIGLR) and the Great Lakes Integrated Sciences and Assessments (GLISA), I have focused on flood mitigation strategies in the Midwest. My work emphasizes strengthening community capacity to withstand and recover from climate-related disasters through nature-based solutions and community-led resilience planning approaches.

📊 Policy Analysis

I evaluate environmental policies through an equity lens, analyzing their impacts on diverse communities and recommending evidence-based improvements to advance both environmental and social justice goals. My focus areas include Indigenous rights and sovereignty, Great Lakes water quality (particularly the Line 5 pipeline controversy), drinking water access, and energy justice.
